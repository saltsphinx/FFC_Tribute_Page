Alan Watts

In tribute to the man best known for his interpretations of eastern beliefs to a western audience

A timeline of Alan Watts' amazing accomplishments:

  • 1915 - Born in Chislehurst, United Kingdom
  • 1930s - Chooses Buddhism over his Anglican Christianity beliefs
  • 1931 - Becomes a secretary at the London Buddhist Lodge
  • 1936 - Publishes his first book, The Spirit of Zen
  • 1938 - Marries his first wife, has a child and moves to New York, United States, where he began Zen training
  • 1945 - Is ordained as an Episcopal priest
  • 1950s - Resigns as a priest, moves to San Francisco, California, and joins the American Academy of Asian Studies faculty
  • 1953 - Leaves the faculty, and becomes a volunteer programmer the radio station KPFA. This is where he began to develop an audience
  • 1957 - Publishes his best known book, The Way of Zen
  • 1959 - Begins recording for a television series for KQED named Eastern Wisdom and Modern Life
  • 1973 - Dies at the age of 58 of natural causes in Druid Heights, Califorina

"Meditation is like dancing, when you dance you are not aiming to get the end of the dance/song, you are dancing for the sake of dancing, you enjoy it. Likewise you don’t meditate to reach “satori” enlightenment, you meditate for the sake of meditation, you enjoy it."

-- Alan Watts
